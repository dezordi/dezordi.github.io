About

I'm interested in science, evolution, virology, workflows, and the development of bioinformatics tools. I have a degree in Biotechnology but my expertise is in Bioinformatics. Currently, I'm a Ph.D. Candidate on Sciences at Fundação Oswaldo Cruz (FioCruz), bioinformatician volunteer at Rede Genômica FioCruz (RGF) (Fiocruz Genomic Network) and work as Bioinformatician Analyst at Hospital Israelita Albert Einstein (HIAE), Brazil.


Bioinformatician

My work on HIAE involves the development and application of bioinformatics workflows to analyze human genomics and code development to automatize tasks on AWS and through use of APIs. To do it, I mainly develop with Workflow Development Language (WDL), Amazon Web Services (AWS), Docker, Git, Shell Script and Python in agile teams following the Scrum methodology.

During my Ph.D., I worked with genomic surveillance of SARS-CoV-2. I built the ViralFlow - with other members of RGF - a workflow that performs a set of viral genomic analyses from sequencing raw data. Moreover, I published key studies to understand several aspects of SARS-CoV-2 evolution during the pandemic in Brazil as well as I collaborate with several studies mainly on the biology of mosquito vectors of arbovirus and paleovirology.

To perform my Ph.D. research I worked with Multi-user Linux servers, PBS queue system, Docker, Singularity, Python, R and Shell Script.
